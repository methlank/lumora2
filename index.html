What is Lumora 2?

A community-driven initiative for lasting change.

"We don't raise awareness. We show up, roll up our sleeves, and build things that last."

Lumora 2 is a university student-led initiative improving educational infrastructure in underprivileged rural schools. This year — a full science lab, a water tank, and essential repairs across two schools in the Mahawa Educational Zone. Everything funded by sponsors like you. Everything built by our 50+ volunteers.

🔬
Science lab + classroom
Full construction at Siyambalangamuwa Junior School — the first lab the school has ever had.
Construction · June 2026
💧
Water tank installation
Reliable water access at Atharagalla Junior School with tripod structure.
Installation · June 2026
⚡
Repairs & improvements
Ceiling fans, tap line repairs across both schools for a better environment.
Throughout June

Our Journey

Where it all started.

Lumora 2 builds on the foundation of Lumora 1. Here is the story of how university students stopped talking about change and started building it.

Lumora 1 showed us that with determination and community, university students can create real, lasting change in the lives of children who need it most.

— Lumora 1 Organizing Committee

100%
Community-funded

Lumora 1 — 2025

The project that started it all.

Through a well-coordinated donation campaign, funds and essential items were collected with the support of students, faculty, and sponsors — all on a budget of over LKR 400,000.

Lumora 2025 delivered safe drinking water improvements, distributed dry ration packs to around 30 families, and provided stationery packs to children. Most importantly, it gave university students a platform to engage directly with communities and contribute with empathy and responsibility.

About Us

The organisation behind Lumora.

Lumora 2 is an initiative of the IEEE Power & Energy Society Student Branch Chapter at SLIIT. Here's who we are.

About IEEE

The world's leading engineering organisation.

IEEE — the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers — is the leading global association for professionals in technology and engineering. Since its establishment in 1884, it has grown to over 400,000 members across more than 160 nations.

IEEE organises international conferences, facilitates the exchange of research and technical advancements, and fosters collaboration among experts. At its core, IEEE is committed to driving technological innovation for the benefit of humanity.

Its primary purpose: to support the advancement of technology and ensure the free flow of knowledge, contributing to the betterment of society and global progress.

About IEEE PES

Power & Energy Society.

The Power & Energy Society (PES) offers an unparalleled platform for the exchange of cutting-edge advancements in the electric power industry. PES encourages innovation and best practices across all areas of electric power and generation.

Through technical conferences, workshops, and the publication of research materials, PES ensures professionals and researchers have the platforms and resources to stay at the forefront of innovation and drive the power and energy sector forward.

PES of SLIIT

Where Lumora was born.

SLIIT is a leading higher education institution in Sri Lanka offering programs in engineering and technology. The IEEE PES Student Branch Chapter at SLIIT gives students a platform to engage with the latest in power engineering and energy systems.

Through events, workshops, technical sessions, and research initiatives, PES SLIIT connects students with industry professionals and cultivates a community passionate about the future of energy.

Lumora 2 is one such initiative — channelling that energy into building real change in rural communities.
